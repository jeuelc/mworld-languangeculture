Bullfighting and identity

 
click here click here

Say 'bullfighting' in Spain and the debate starts. Some people love it, others hate it. In some places it's banned, in other places people live for it.

Bullfighting is a very old tradition that dates back to prehistoric times when humans engaged in ritual fights with animals to prove the superiority of humans over the animal kingdom. Nowadays, it involves a bullfighter killing a bull after performing a number of moves, and it remains a popular spectacle in some parts of Spain. But it is non-existent or even banned in other parts.

Bullfighting has become an icon of Spanishness, to the point where you can see some Spanish flags with the image of a bull in the centre, replacing the Spanish coat of arms. In Catalonia, where the Catalan government wanted to show they are more Catalan than Spanish, bullfighting was banned in 2011 (mind you in French Catalonia, bullfighting is a way to show your Catalan identity!).
