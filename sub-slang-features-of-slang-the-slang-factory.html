We often use a few time-worn tricks to create slang:

Shortening: take stuffy old words and squish them up to make them shorter and easier to say.

Affixation: add extra sounds or syllables at the start, end or middle of words. It makes them longer but also injects new life into them!

Blending: take two or more words and smear them together to create new ones.

Remodelling: same old words, spoken in a new-fangled way.
