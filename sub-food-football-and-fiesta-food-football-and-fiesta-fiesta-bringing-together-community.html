Fiesta ... bringing together community

Every Spanish city, big or small, has its own festival. There are hundreds! Some are famous worldwide and attract many tourists.

In Buñol, people throw tomatoes at each other for hours; in Pamplona, they run for three very long minutes in front of six bulls charging at high speed; in Valencia, huge papier mâché sculptures are erected, all of which, except for the best one, are later burned in a big party; in Sabucedo they round up wild horses and cut their manes; in Catalonia people stand on the shoulders of others to form human towers up to 10 storeys high called castellets (little castles). Imagine being the person at the top!

These festivities are more than mere fun (which they can be though maybe not running in front of a charging bull!) They also express belonging to a community over time, as you engage in festivities undertaken by your ancestor's centuries ago. For newcomers, being involved is a way to belong to the community.
