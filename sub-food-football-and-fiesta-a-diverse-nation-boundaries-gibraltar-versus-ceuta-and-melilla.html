Gibraltar versus Ceuta & Melilla

Way down on the southern tip of the Iberian Peninsula there is a port city called Gibraltar. It is famous for the rock, populated by monkeys, that towers over the city. Gibraltar belonged to Spain until 1704. That year, Spain lost control over the territory during a war and since then Gibraltar has formed part of the United Kingdom, 1750 kilometres away. For Spain, however, Gibraltar is Spanish and they repeatedly ask for ownership to be returned.

On the other side of the Mediterranean Sea can be found two small cities called Ceuta and Melilla. These cities are surrounded by Morocco, but belong to Spain. Like Spain with Gibraltar, Morocco claims these two cities as part of its nation. Spain refuses to give them up. It seems logical that Gibraltar should be Spanish and Ceuta and Melilla should belong to Morocco, but history triumphs over geography sometimes!
