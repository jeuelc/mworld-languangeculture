Panels

Panels are the individual frames or drawings that make up a comic. They are used to show how a story develops over time, like a film strip. English newspaper comics, such as Dilbert or Garfield, often have three or four panels that you read from left-to-right. In Japanese newspapers, panels are arranged vertically and you read from top-to-bottom.

Comic book and manga magazine stories often have many more panels per page. The stories are longer and the layout is more creative.

Because Japanese books are read from right-to-left and the writing is often top to bottom, sometimes manga look strange when they're translated into English.

When manga first became popular in English-speaking countries, publishers 'flipped' the manga so that it read from left-to-right. This created a 'mirror-world', where designs on clothing were backwards, and almost everyone was left-handed! Today, most translated manga are printed in their original format, and fans learn to start at the 'back' of the book.
