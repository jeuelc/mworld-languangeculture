Irony

 

When people use irony, they use language to express a meaning that is the opposite of their actual meaning (the literal meaning). Nicknames often have an ironic twist to them: Shorty (for someone really tall), Curly (for someone with dead-straight hair) and Blue or Bluey (for a redhead).

Irony is at the basis of teenager slang. Someone who is particularly attractive might be described as 'scum'. Words like 'wicked', 'vicious', 'sick', 'rancid', 'filthy' and 'putrid' can describe things that are exceptionally good. Of course there's always a rapid turnover of 'awesome words' and you and your friends will have your favourite expression!

A slangy bit of grammar with a nice dose of irony is also the way teenagers often throw a stranded 'not' at the end of the sentence. 'What a totally amazing, excellent discovery... NOT!!' In this sense you're making it clear that you're being sarcastic. This way of using not first appeared in the 1990s, but it's alive and well today!

Humour often springs from surprise. By unexpectedly reversing the meaning of a word or phrase, your timing and use of slang can often be funny.
