When Castilian became the national language, other languages didnt disappear. Although many people adopted Spanish, many still spoke their mother tongue at home or with friends. Despite restrictions or bans placed on speaking Catalan by different governments, there are now about 10 million speakers! How did it survive?

Many Catalans feel strongly attached to their language. So they didnt give it up. Also, many Spaniards who moved from poorer regions to Catalonia during the 1950s in search of jobs learnt Catalan because it was seen as a language of wealth and success. Most important, however, is that since the last dictator to ban Catalan died (in 1975), Catalan is now taught in schools.
