Visual Conventions

You can convey a lot about your character using visual conventions. For example, eyes are often drawn very large in manga style, while noses and mouths are much smaller. Eye size and shape is used to demonstrate a character's feelings, and is often considered more important than the position of the mouth. Eyes are drawn lower on the face for small children. As people age, the position of their eyes becomes higher.

Facial expressions also change the position of features on the face. If you wanted to draw a character laughing, for example, you'd draw them with higher eyebrows and an upwards curved mouth. Characters who are angry have lower eyebrows and a downwards curved mouth. Crying characters have eyebrows that are low on the sides, and a lower mouth.

The colours you choose for your character can also convey meaning. Green hair might mean fortune, purple hair wisdom, pink hair kindness, red hair energy, and silver hair intelligence!
