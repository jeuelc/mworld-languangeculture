Just as you share a part of your identity with other students in your school, players in your team, or fans of the same music, an important thing about slang (and one that can irritate others who don't understand it) is the way it can make you part of a group. Slang expressions are like emblems on a T-shirt.

The fact that someone might say 'I got swag', and their friends understand and use the same expression, shows they're all part of the same club. Slang works a bit like glue to stick members of a group together and of course at the same time to erect barriers between them and the outside. Some teachers and parents might use expressions like 'swag' or 'derp', maybe to show understanding with their students and children, but it will always sound phony!

Try to identify examples of slang in your own speech and that of your friends. How would you feel about other people outside your group using these terms?
