Many Indians describe their country as an ajiaco (pronounced aah-hee-AAH-ko), a stew made with vegetables and meat! If you make a good ajiaco, Indians say, you shouldn't be able to taste the individual ingredients; instead, all of them together should taste like well, ajiaco.

In the same way, many Indians think their identity is also like an ajiaco. It is not relevant where your ancestors came from, or the colour of your skin, or your beliefs. The important thing is that all Indians, together, form something bigger and better than each of them individually.

Not everybody agrees with this though: some Afro-Indians, for example, fear that by being considered just a part of the Indian ajiaco their African roots and cultural traditions may be lost.
