A lot of the story in manga is told through pictures without needing any words. How do you think they do that? It isn't just by drawing the action!

Manga artists use special symbols to show a character's emotions. This is sometimes called the 'visual language' of manga.

Some commonly used symbols are sweat drops and tear drops. A droplet above the eye or running down the side of the face represents a sweaty forehead - the character is stressed or physically tired.

Another common symbol is the cruciform or 'cross-popping' vein. This represents the veins popping out of the head of a character to show they are really angry!

Red cheeks, or in black-and-white manga, cross-hatching, represent blushing and embarrassment.
