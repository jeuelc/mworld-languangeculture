When Robin says to Batman, 'Holy bargain basements, Batman!' this is alliteration. Alliteration is a repeated consonant sound in a series of words. Slang often involves alliteration, as in 'dentured dandy': a rather rude reference to an older person. (Dentures are false teeth!)

There are oodles of slang words for bodily functions. Here are some expressions for vomiting which make good use of alliteration:

  • pash the porcelain princess
  • launch (one's) lunch
  • liquid laugh
  • hiccup from hell
  • tango with the toilet
  • vomit your victuals
  • greet your guts

Something that is related to alliteration is when you get whole bunches of words with similar meanings that are linked by the same starting consonant. Think of all those mild swearwords that begin with 'bl' like 'blimey', 'blast', 'blow', 'blessed', 'bleeding', 'blinking', 'blooming', 'blinding' and 'blasted'! Can you think of other groups of similar-sounding words that hang around meanings in this way?
