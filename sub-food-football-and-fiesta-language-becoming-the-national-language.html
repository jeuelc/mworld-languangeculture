Becoming the national language

Spanish is also known as 'Castilian' because it originated in the kingdom of Castile during the Middle Ages. If it's the language of Castile, how did it become Spanish? Would you believe it partly came about because of a wedding?

When Ferdinand, king of Aragon, and Isabella, queen of Castile, married in 1478, there was only one royal court in Castile. So the nobles who spoke other languages adopted Castilian so they could talk to one another at the court. Writers, who depended on nobles to support them, then wrote in Castilian. The Castilian language became prestigious, while other languages were seen as local, not national.
