Standing Out and Having Fun

The slang of all age groups is playful. Look at how people bounce their slangy creations off each other. It's a way of creating a sense that you get on with someone. You've probably got a nickname your friends use. Perhaps it's a shortened version of your name?

The curious phrase the 'cat's pyjamas' was a fad expression of the 1920s that bred many copy-cat constructions: 'the cat's whiskers', 'the cat's meow', 'the cat's eyebrows', 'the cat's ankle', 'tonsils', 'adenoids', even 'the cat's cufflinks', 'galoshes' and 'roller-skates' all meaning 'outstanding'.

Ten years later came 'the bee's knees', with the added catchiness of a playful rhyme. Then came heaps of expressions combining animals with body parts or clothing, and some were really bizarre! 'The eel's heel', 'the elephant's eyebrows' or 'instep', 'the gnat's eyebrows', 'the ant's eyebrows' are just a few.

Nowadays, even if you're not living in the tropics, you've probably heard, 'See you later, alligator! In a while, crocodile!' These sorts of expressions show how inventive slang can be and the fun people have in creating new expressions.
