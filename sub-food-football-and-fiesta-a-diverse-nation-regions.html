During the Middle Ages, as the Christians took back territory ruled by the Muslims, some new kingdoms were formed: Castile, Leon, Portugal, Aragon, Navarre, Valencia and Mallorca. Catalonia was ruled first by the counts of Barcelona and later the kings of Aragon. The Muslim-controlled area of Al-Andalus became modern-day Andalusia. Mostly these kingdoms had their own languages and laws and saw themselves as different from the other kingdoms. Spain as a nation didnt exist.

So, how did Spain become a nation? Weddings and wars, thats how.

When marriages took place between royal families of different kingdoms, the kingdoms merged; for example Aragon and Castile and, later, Spain and Portugal. But wars played an important part too. In 1640 Aragon, Valencia, Catalonia and Portugal rebelled against king Philip IV, but only Portugal became independent. As a result it is a separate country while Aragon, Catalonia and Valencia are now three of the 17 regions that make up Spain.
