How does a speech bubble's shape tell us about a characters emotions?

The shape of the speech bubble helps to convey a character's tone of voice. In a novel, the author can always write "she boomed" or "he whispered", but in a manga, the only words are those spoken by the characters. So visual means like font size and shape, and speech bubble size and shape are used to show how loud someone is speaking, or the tone of voice they're using.

Thought bubbles are also used to display what a character is thinking or feeling. These often look more like clouds, to show that they are thinking quietly, not talking out loud.

The same word can take on a new meaning when it is presented in different shaped bubbles. Jagged edges can show a strong feeling, like shouting in surprise. Wobbly edges can show an element of fear, like being surprised by a ghost. And thought bubbles with little clouds can show speechlessness.
