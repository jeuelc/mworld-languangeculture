What do you think the coolest thing in your country is?

When describing the economy of a country, people talk about its 'Gross Domestic Product' (GDP). This is the value of goods and services produced by that country. Japan's GDP was very strong in the 1980s and '90s. Recently, people have started to describe Japan's 'Gross National Cool' (GNC)! Hello Kitty is an example of GNC. In 2008, Hello Kitty was named Japan's official ambassador of tourism to China and Hong Kong, the first fictional character in this role.

Much of Japan's GNC is related to manga/anime. 'Cool Japan' as a brand has been adopted by the Japanese government. The Ministry for Economy, Trade and Industry even has a Cool Japan Promotion Office!

You can see the influence of manga across the world. There are manga cafes throughout Asia and Europe, and American and European comics have been influenced by manga. Some surveys show that more kids around the world recognise Japanese characters like Pikachu and Mario than Mickey Mouse!
