Metaphor

 
click here click here

Scratch the surface of many slang expressions and you'll find metaphor. When you use metaphorical language you are describing one kind of thing in terms of another.

Take these two examples: dog and tank. If you describe another person as a dog, you're saying they behave or look a bit like the animal. To describe a person as a tank is to paint a picture of someone who resembles an armoured vehicle.

Sometimes the metaphor is dead and buried. 'Dude' probably comes from the slangy expression duds meaning 'clothes'. The original dudes in the 18th century were men who dressed in a fashionable and showy manner but now it just means 'man'.

Clearly people aren't dogs, tanks or clothes, so a metaphor is a kind of lie! But by using these expressions, you're making a connection between people (the figurative meaning) and things (the literal meaning) and a lot of the colour and vibrancy of slanguage comes from such metaphors.
