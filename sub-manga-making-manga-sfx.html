SFX

 

Comics are famous for their sound effects (SFX) like "POW!" or "BAM!" Japanese has even more SFX than English, many of which are unique.

SFX in Japanese manga don't just express action sounds. They also tell you how a character is feeling, what the background sounds like, and there is even one for silence (shiiin)! There are SFX to express the sound of a pencil rolling across a table (koro koro), a flying airship (guoooooo) or biting into an apple (baku!) among many other weird and wonderful examples.

In contrast to speech, which is normally typed, SFX are usually hand drawn, and are seen as part of the artwork. Because there are so many SFX in Japanese, and because they are often seen as part of the art, they are very difficult to translate. Sometimes, they aren't translated at all.
